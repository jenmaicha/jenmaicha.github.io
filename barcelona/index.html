Things to Do

Donkey Tours

This is a pay-what-you-want walking tour company. I did this itinerary (but in Spanish). It was a nice way to explore the old town, and the guide was a good storyteller, too. English-language tours start at 11 a.m. in Placa Nova, near the cathedral. Allow 2.5 to 3 hours for the tour. https://donkeytours.es/en/old-town/

Born Center for Culture and Memory

A free museum and cultural space that focuses on the history of the Born neighborhood. The centerpiece is a large excavation of the ruins of a portion of the neighborhood that was destroyed during the War of the Spanish Succession (early 1700s). http://elbornculturaimemoria.barcelona.cat/en/the-center/

Museu d'Història de Barcelona

A municipal history museum that runs/maintains heritage sites around the city, including ancient Roman sites, the medieval Jewish quarter, the steps where (according to legend) Ferdinand and Isabella received Columbus when he returned from his first trip to the "New World," and a Spanish Civil War-era bomb shelter. http://ajuntament.barcelona.cat/museuhistoria/en/muhba-placa-del-rei

Basilica de Santa Maria del Mar

Lovely, unadorned Catalan Gothic church in El Born. http://www.santamariadelmarbarcelona.org/home/

Parc de la Ciutadella

Formal park with a lively atmosphere. Note that the Catalan parliament building is within the park grounds.

Port Cable Car and Montjuïc Cable Car

You can take a cable car from the beach up to Montjuïc, walk a bit, and then catch another cable car up to the top of Montjuïc, where there is a castle (see below) and presumably some very nice views. I've never done this. Check this page for information: https://barcelonalowdown.com/barcelona-cable-cars/

Montjuïc Castle

I've never been here, but this is what's at the end of the pair of cable cars. http://ajuntament.barcelona.cat/castelldemontjuic/en

Beachfront walkway

There's a nice beachfront area from the W hotel to Port Olimpic.

Things to Eat

Dinner starts late in Barcelona, so don't be put off if a restaurant looks empty at 6pm.

You'll be around for lunch and afternoon snacking. It's easy to find small things to eat throughout the day. Some common local foods:

Pan con tomate is a classic Catalan snack of grilled bread brushed with olive oil and crushed tomato. Vermouth is served on tap. Cava (a sparkling wine) is another local/regional specialty. Roasted chestnuts are a fall specialty. Get them and sweet potatoes from street vendors. Patatas bravas are roasted potatoes with a spicy sauce.

Bormuth

Friendly, inexpensive place for vermouth, drinks, and tapas near the Born Center for Culture and Memory. The lentil salad was light and refreshing. https://www.yelp.com/biz/bormuth-barcelona

Les Dues Sicilies

Pizza by the slice. Several locations. https://www.facebook.com/lesduesiciliesbcn/

La Fábrica

Empanadas! http://www.lafabrica-bcn.com/

Al Sur

A nice, relaxing cafe with several locations. I used to go to the one on Carrer dels Carders to write. http://www.alsurcafe.com/

Santa Caterina market

Various food vendors. Their website is only in Catalan though.
